Version 1.12

Your character code now carries the rest of your sheet, so a virtual tabletop can show it in full.

  • Exhaustion states travel with your code. A tabletop that reads it can show all four, let you toggle them, and grey out the attribute each one shuts down.
  • Your origin abilities now come across with their full rules text, so you can read what they do without opening the creator.
  • Origin, archetype and temperament descriptions are included, along with your temperament's exhaustion clause.
  • Bonds now carry the bond type's name and description rather than just a label.

Codes are longer again, around 9 kB. Still fine to copy and paste. If you exported a code before this version it will still work, you just won't get exhaustion tracking until you export a fresh one.

Version 1.11

Character codes can now be read by a virtual tabletop.

  • Your code now includes your finished numbers: attributes, skills, Spirit and Supply maxima, tech level, your talents and your equipment with damage, injury, protection and qualities.
  • This means another tool can display your character without needing a copy of the rules. Paste your code in once and it knows who you are.
  • Everything you change during play — Spirit spent, injuries taken, truths written, gear discharged — can be carried back here by pasting the code in again. Nothing is lost in the trip.

Codes roughly doubled in length. Older codes still load normally, and codes made from this version onward still work in older copies of the creator.
